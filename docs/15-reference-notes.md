# Reference Notes

## Study pillars
- Synced notation and practice workflow.
- Tuning and capo-aware guitar logic.
- Guitar-aligned audio-video-score data.
- Percussive fingerstyle interpretation as product identity.

## Must copy
- Tight sync behavior between notation and playback.
- Looping and slowdown that feel immediate.
- Guitar-first controls like tuning and capo.
- Honest confidence display and review workflow.
- Workspace-first design over marketing-first design.

## Must avoid
- Generic AI app look.
- Marketing-first interface.
- Hiding uncertainty.
- Starting with full AI complexity before shell and workflow exist.
- Pretending V1 has full percussion intelligence.

## Study order
1. Soundslice main site and features.
2. Soundslice slowdown and recording workflows.
3. Flat tuning, tabs, and custom instrument docs.
4. Guitar Pro product framing.
5. GAPS dataset and aligned guitar research material.

## Product alignment note
The product should be framed as percussive fingerstyle-first, but V1 should only promise basic percussion support through markers and technique labels, not full classification.
